A note about prototyping

Now, where does prototyping come in in our Design Process. These are 5 stages of design process. 1. Discovery: Understand the problem at hand, brainstorm & design brief
2. Exploration: several design directions have been explored, evaluated and eliminated.
3. Design: single design direction has been explored and validated
4. Prototype: interaction prototype has been built and validated
5. Test & Refine: single design workflow has been specified in detail.
Why   Prototype?

Adobe Design

  • Ideas are vague
  • Explore multiple options
  • Ideas need evaluation
  • Ideas when dynamic are more impactful
Ideas are vague: when you’re uncertain about the goal that the interaction could achieve, it is good to prototype and give it in the hand of users.
When you have multiple potentially good options to pick from, it is a good idea to prototype and test it to validate your options.
When you’re unsure whether a workflow is intuitive or not, you can build and validate your idea Takes too long to build for production
Not all ideas can be statically expressed. Playing with the prototype helps gain insights into potentially improving your ideas

Prototype   What?

Adobe Design

  • Tool behavior
  • Comparison between options
  • Functionality/workflow of a component/feature
  • Mouse interactions/Direct manipulation
  • Animation/Timing
  • Visibility/Legibility
To summarize the what we build and test with prototypes, we can build all of these and more.

So as we prototyped, we started understanding what to prototype, what features can be validated with a simple deck or story and which features should be left to engineering team. To summarize, It is important to have clear objectives when you start building a prototype. Depending on time and feasibility, we can achieve our objectives with prototypes of different kind. Based on need, we can choose to build a paper prototype, a click through PDF, an interactive prototype on XD, or an engineering prototype.
To sum it up, you should build an Experience Prototype if you want a POC for testing workflows and interaction.
